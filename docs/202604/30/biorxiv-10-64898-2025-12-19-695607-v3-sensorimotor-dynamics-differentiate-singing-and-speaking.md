## 摘要
歌唱和说话在临床上常表现出解离现象——口吃患者可以流畅地歌唱，而中风导致失语症及言语表达障碍的个体却能在歌唱中流畅地表达句子——但这种延续了几个世纪的临床现象的神经机制仍不清楚。我们记录了神经外科患者在以歌唱或说话方式产生匹配句子时的颅内脑电图 (iEEG)，以毫秒和毫米级精度采样双半球的脑活动。在发音过程中，高频活动 (70–150 Hz) 在两种行为中呈现相反的侧化模式：歌唱激活右主导的体感运动皮层 (SMC)，而说话激活左主导的区域。相位-振幅耦合分析显示，mu 频段（约 10 Hz）的相位在左侧 SMC 内以通道特异、空间交错的结构局部地组织高频活动，这种结构在两种行为中均得以保留。说话时的 mu 频段同步性表现为早期的左侧主导模式，而歌唱则表现出左体感运动皮层内及双侧运动皮层之间同步性的逐步增强，支持渐进的双半球间协作招募。频域 Granger–Geweke 因果分析揭示，在言语开始时，左初级体感皮层驱动双侧运动皮层。相较之下，在歌唱中，对运动皮层的控制依赖于双半球。歌唱和说话通过不同的招募动态，激活了共享的 mu 耦合体感运动基础。

## Abstract
Singing and speaking often dissociate clinically--people who stutter can sing fluently, and individuals with aphasia and speech output problems from stroke may express sentences fluently in song--yet the neural mechanisms of this centuries-old clinical phenomenon remain unclear. We recorded intracranial EEG while neurosurgical patients produced matched sentences by singing or speaking, sampling millimeter- and millisecond-scale activity across both hemispheres. During articulation, high-frequency activity (70-150 Hz) lateralized oppositely across behaviors, with right-dominant sensorimotor cortex (SMC) activation for singing and left-dominant activation for speaking. Phase-amplitude coupling revealed that mu-band ([~]10 Hz) phase locally organized HFA in left SMC in a channel-specific, spatially interdigitated architecture preserved across both behaviors. Mu-band synchrony in speaking showed an early left-led pattern, whereas singing exhibited a ramping of synchrony within the left sensorimotor cortex and between the two motor cortices, supporting progressive interhemispheric recruitment. Frequency-domain Granger- Geweke causality revealed that the left primary somatosensory cortex drives both motor cortices at speech onset. In contrast in singing, control over motor cortices relied on both hemispheres. Singing and speaking engage a shared mu-coupled sensorimotor substrate through distinct recruitment dynamics.
