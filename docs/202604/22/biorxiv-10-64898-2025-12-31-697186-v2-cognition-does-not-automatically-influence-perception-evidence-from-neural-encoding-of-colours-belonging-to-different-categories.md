## 摘要
支持认知对知觉产生早期高级影响模型的最有力证据来自脑电图（EEG）。研究报告指出，与浅蓝和深蓝特征变化相比，希腊语使用者（他们有两个基本蓝色词汇 ghalazio/ble）在处理浅绿和深绿特征变化时表现出增强的早期、前注意阶段加工。在本研究的三个实验中，我们系统地重新评估了这一证据，并检验了另一种模型：人类 EEG 的早期差异波主要反映对比度适应现象。我们使用经典的 oddball 范式，交替呈现颜色和/或亮度及色度对比系统性不同的标准刺激与反常刺激。然后，我们计算视觉失配负波（visual mismatch negativity，vMMN），这一指标被认为反映前注意特征变化加工和预测编码，是从 EEG 数据中通过标准刺激与任务无关反常刺激的脑活动差异得到的。我们的实验结果表明：（1）vMMN 由对比度适应驱动，仅在刺激间存在对比度差异时可观察到，而对于在对比度上匹配的类别不同色调则不可靠；（2）在具有两种基本蓝色类别的俄语使用者中，绿色与蓝色相关的差异波之间并无可靠差异，这些差异波同样由对比度而非类别属性驱动。我们的发现对知觉与认知之间的关系争论具有重要意义，因为缺乏早期类别效应显示，预示着否定了预测存在沃尔夫假说类型的前注意认知影响于知觉的模型。

意义陈述：沃尔夫假说认为语言的基本类别会从根本上改变人们对世界的感知。一些最具说服力的证据来自电生理反应，显示不同语言使用者之间在基本颜色类别数量上存在早期差异。这些脑反应被认为是早期、前注意加工和预测编码的可靠标志。在当前的多实验研究中，我们提供证据表明，对颜色的早期电生理差异反映的是色调、饱和度和亮度对比及这种对比的适应，而非语言类别。这意味着支持早期、前注意类别化颜色知觉的证据已被显著削弱。

## Abstract
The firmest evidence in favour of models that posit early high-level influences of cognition on perception comes from electroencephalography (EEG). Enhanced early, pre-attentive processing of light and dark blue feature changes compared to light and dark green changes was reported in Greek speakers, who have two basic terms for  blue (ghalazio/ble). In the present three-experiment study, we systematically re-evaluate this evidence and test an alternative model that the early difference waves in the human EEG instead mainly reflect contrast adaptation phenomena. We use the same classical oddball paradigm presenting alternating standards and deviants that systematically differ in colour and/or luminance and chromatic contrast. We then calculate the visual mismatch negativity (vMMN), a putative index of pre-attentive feature change processing and predictive coding derived from EEG data, by subtracting the activity elicited by standards from that elicited by task-irrelevant deviants. Our experiments demonstrate the following: 1) vMMN is driven by contrast adaptation, being observable only in the presence of contrast differences between the stimuli and not reliably observed for categorically different hues equated in contrast; 2) there is no reliable difference between green- and blue-related difference waves in speakers (Russian) with two basic blue colour categories, the difference waves, again, being driven by contrast rather than their categorical content. Our findings are highly significant for the debate concerning the interface between perception and cognition, as the absence of early categorical effects speaks against models that predict Whorfian-type pre-attentive cognitive influences on perception.

Significance statementThe Whorfian hypothesis posits that basic language categories alter ones perception of the world in a fundamental manner. Some of the most compelling evidence in favour of this hypothesis came from electrophysiological responses that indicated early differences between speakers of languages with different number of basic colour categories. The brain response taken as an indicator of these differences was considered to be a robust marker of early, pre-attentive processing and predictive coding. In the current multi-experiment study, we present evidence that early electrophysiological differences to colour reflect signatures of hue, saturation and luminance contrast and adaptation to this contrast, rather than of linguistic categories. This means that evidence in favour of early, pre-attentive categorical colour perception has been significantly eroded.
