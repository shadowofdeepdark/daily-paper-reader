## 摘要
工作记忆（Working Memory, WM）的表征分布于整个大脑，可以灵活地被调用以最有效地指导行为。例如，当工作记忆任务需要精细的视觉细节时，信息可能在视觉皮层中的表征更为强烈；而当可以准备特定反应时，信息可能在运动皮层中的表征更为突出。如果工作记忆驱动目标导向的行动，我们或许还可以预期这类依赖任务的信号会传播到实现工作记忆指令的外周效应器。因此，诸如凝视偏向等动眼特征可以追踪简单的视觉空间工作记忆特征，但其功能灵活性尚不明确。本研究检验了一个假设：工作记忆内容会根据行为需求自适应地分布在整个神经系统中。我们考察了眼动和手部运动的模式是否都能表达视觉工作记忆的刺激特征，以及这种活动的分布是否会随任务情境而变化。在延迟回忆任务中，我们操纵人类被试报告记忆的方式：他们要么画出一条线，要么调整一个可见的响应轮以匹配记忆中的方向。通过连续的眼动和手写笔轨迹追踪，我们发现，在工作记忆保持阶段，记忆的方向可以从凝视和手部运动中的微小变化中被解码出来。此外，这种解码的强度随反应形式而变化。在轮盘条件（相对于绘画条件）下，凝视模式更好地追踪了记忆的特征；而在绘画条件（相对于轮盘条件）下，手部运动的表现更佳。因此，视觉编码的工作记忆内容可能被自适应地分配到与任务相关的运动效应器上，根据行为需求平衡外周活动中的工作记忆表征。

## Abstract
Working memory (WM) representations that are distributed across the brain can be flexibly recruited to best guide behavior. For instance, information may be represented relatively more strongly in visual cortex when a WM task requires fine visual detail, or more strongly in motor cortex when a specific response can be prepared. If WM drives goal-oriented actions, we might also expect such task-dependent signals to propagate to the peripheral effectors that realize WM commands. Accordingly, oculomotor signatures like gaze biases can track simple visuo-spatial WM features, but their functional flexibility is unclear. Here, we test the idea that WM content is adaptively distributed across the nervous system according to behavioral demands. We ask whether patterns in both eye and hand movements can express visual WM stimulus features, and whether the distribution of such activity shifts with the task context. In a delayed recall task, we manipulated how human participants reported their memory: they would either draw a line or adjust a visible response wheel to match a remembered orientation. Via continuous eye- and stylus-tracking, we found that remembered orientations were decodable from small inflections in both gaze and hand movements during the WM maintenance period. Moreover, this decoding strength varied by response format. Gaze patterns tracked memorized features relatively better in the wheel condition (vs. draw), while hand movements were better in the draw condition (vs. wheel). Therefore, visually encoded WM contents may be adaptively allocated to task-relevant motor effectors, balancing WM representations across peripheral activity according to behavioral needs.
