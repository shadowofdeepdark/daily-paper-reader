## 摘要
许多认知过程依赖于随着信息逐步呈现而进行整合，以构建有意义的解释。以往研究表明，尤其在语言领域中，存在分级和渐进的语境效应，但当结构化输入出现在一致的序列中时，不同符号领域的语境整合是否表现出相似的时间特征仍不明确。二十七名参与者在记录事件相关电位（ERP）的同时，处理了旨在在词汇、代数和图形领域间结构上可比的、一致的四元素序列。

在250–500毫秒区间内，预定义的中央-顶叶兴趣区的平均振幅随序列位置系统性地增加（p < .001）。领域×位置的交互作用未达到显著性（p = .056），这表明各领域间的增加速率仅存在轻微差异。后续分析显示，相较于早期的位置，最终位置的振幅增加更大（p < .001）。进一步的分析结果表明，该效应呈广泛的中央-顶叶分布，顶叶区域的后期振幅最大，而中央区域的递增效应最为明显。

这些结果表明，语境整合是渐进的，但在不同的序列位置间并不均匀，在序列完成时增幅最显著。词汇、代数和图形领域间表现出大体可比的特征，暗示在该时间范围内的后期中央-顶叶活动可能反映了一种相对领域通用的过程，支持从结构化输入中形成有意义的理解。

## Abstract
Many cognitive processes depend on integrating information as it becomes available to construct meaningful interpretations. Prior work has shown graded and incremental context effects, especially in language, but it remains less clear whether contextual integration exhibits a comparable temporal profile across symbolic domains when structured input is examined within congruent sequences. Twenty-seven participants processed congruent four-element sequences designed to be structurally comparable across lexical, algebraic, and graphical domains while event-related potentials were recorded.

In the 250-500 ms interval, mean amplitudes increased systematically with sequence position within a predefined centro-parietal region of interest (p < .001). The Domain x Position interaction did not reach significance (p = .056), suggesting only modest variation in the rate of increase across domains. A follow-up analysis showed that the increase to the final position was larger than earlier increases (p < .001). Additional analyses indicated a broadly centro-parietal distribution, with maximal late amplitudes over parietal sites and the clearest graded increase over central sites.

These findings indicate that contextual integration was progressive but not uniform across sequence positions, with the strongest increase at sequence completion. The presence of a broadly comparable profile across lexical, algebraic, and graphical domains suggests that late centro-parietal activity in this time range may reflect a relatively domain-general process supporting meaningful interpretation from structured input.
